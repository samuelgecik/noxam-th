PU Coating vs Epoxy Coating — What's the Difference?

Epoxy coating is known for its hardness and chemical resistance, which is why it's often used in industrial environments. But hardness also brings challenges: epoxy is rigid and can crack under impact, it yellows under UV light, and most importantly — epoxy has a long curing time. This often means several days of downtime, which can be costly for production facilities.

PU coating, on the other hand, offers a flexible, comfortable, and durable surface that cures quickly. PU can be installed in sections, allowing the floor to be coated without major interruptions. This makes PU coating an ideal solution for spaces where time and continuous operation are critical.
